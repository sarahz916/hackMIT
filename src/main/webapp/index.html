Supplemental learning for everyone


What We Do

  • Provide supplemental instruction to bridge the education gap between students of different income-levels
  • Connect students with their peers based on shared subjects, time zones and more to build shared learning communities

Our Mission

COVID-19 has sent millions of children out of classrooms and kept them at home with virtual teaching sessions. Education is typically viewed as an "equal opportunity" for all students; however, this is currently not the case. About half of upper class families say their children have been receiving online instruction since school closed, while only roughly a quarter of lower income families could say the same (Pew Research Center). Though tutoring services are a common form of supplemental education, they can be expensive, further augmenting the income gap in education.

In response to the compromised opportunity and other challenges brought by virtual learning, we developed TutorPod. With TutorPod, we hope to help students by lowering the cost of supplemental instruction through small group sessions, but also develop virtual learning communities to stay connected during these unprecedented times.
